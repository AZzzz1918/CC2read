# 10 — MIXED vs Core B2B Separation Guide

## Rule for Thesis Writing

**Never** merge MIXED_PRODUCTS or non-B2b papers into BDS3_B2B statistics or claims.

## What is Core B2B (95 papers)?

Papers where the **primary** correction source is BDS-3 PPP-B2b broadcast corrections. These papers evaluate B2b performance, use B2b for positioning/timing/ZTD, or describe B2b system design.

## What is MIXED (10 papers)?

Papers that **co-equally** use or compare B2b with other sources:
- Pan Lin 2025: BDS B2b AND Galileo HAS
- Wei Haopeng 2024: Galileo HAS + BDS B2b Helmert combination
- Comparative Broadcast Frameworks: B2b + HAS + MADOCA three-way comparison
- RT ZTD: B2b + HAS + MADOCA for ZTD estimation
- RT Kinematic Orbit LEO: HAS + B2b for LEO orbit
- s10291-024-01730-7: multi-GNSS PPP comparison
- s43020-024-00146-5: BDS augmentation comparison

## What is Non-B2b (4 papers)?

Papers NOT about BDS-3 PPP-B2b:
- Borio 2023: Galileo HAS parser (pure HAS tool)
- Zhou Peiyuan 2024: Galileo HAS initial assessment
- Taro Suzuki 2023: QZSS CLAS antenna evaluation
- Euiho Kim 2022: QZSS CLAS protection level

## Writing Impact

- **Positioning accuracy section:** Use BDS3_B2B papers only (95 papers). Do NOT include MIXED paper results as "B2b positioning accuracy" unless the paper explicitly separates B2b-only results.
- **Multi-system comparison section:** Use MIXED papers (10 papers) as primary evidence. These are the papers that directly compare B2b with HAS/CLAS/RTS.
- **Non-B2b section:** Use CNES + QZSS_CLAS papers (4 papers) to establish what satellite-based PPP looks like outside the BDS-3 ecosystem.
